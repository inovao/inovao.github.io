Quantum superposition is a principle of quantum mechanics. That states any two (or more) quantum states can be added together ("superposed") and the result will be another valid quantum state; and conversely, that every quantum state can be represented as a sum of multiple other distinct states called Entanglements. Entanglements describe the system since each individual particle or piece of data cannot describe the system alone.

Phase 4: Bitcoin Cyber Security. Quantum Computers pose an immense threat to Bitcoin security. Bitcoins are protected by cryptographic protocols similar to factorization. Bitcoin transactions are stored in a distributed ledger system that collates all the deals transacted within a specific time denoted as a “Block” which also contains a cryptographic hash. A block also contains a cryptographic hash of the previous transaction – hence a chain is created lending additional security called a nonce. A nonce (a mathematical value to corroborate a chain). The ability to identify a nonce is called mining. This mining is typically accomplished by brute force computation. The introduction of a quantum computer into this model would give a significant power advantage to mine cryptocurrencies. A quantum computer exploits the inherent parallelism of super-positioning of quantum states (entanglements) and has the potential to increase the solution speed to almost any mathematical problem. A quantum computer would be able to crack these cryptographic hashes with relative ease within 2-2.5 years. In additional a series of long chains (minimally 4) can be created virtually instantaneously by a quantum computer which would mitigate the decentralized nature.
